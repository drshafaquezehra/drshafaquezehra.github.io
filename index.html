Introduction

I am an interdisciplinary humanities researcher whose work explores the intersections of literature, visual culture, education, and emerging technologies. My research examines how stories, images, and multimodal forms shape identity, memory, marginalization, and knowledge production in contemporary society.

Drawing on approaches from stylistics, visual rhetoric, multimodal discourse analysis, and postcolonial studies, I investigate graphic narratives as powerful sites of cultural expression and social inquiry. More recently, my work has expanded to examine artificial intelligence, digital humanities, and the evolving relationship between technology, reading, and higher education.
