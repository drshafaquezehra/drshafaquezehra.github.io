Introduction

I am an interdisciplinary humanities researcher whose work explores the intersections of literature, visual culture, education, and emerging technologies[cite: 1]. My research examines how stories, images, and multimodal forms shape identity, memory, marginalization, and knowledge production in contemporary society[cite: 1].

Drawing on approaches from stylistics, visual rhetoric, multimodal discourse analysis, and postcolonial studies, I investigate graphic narratives as powerful sites of cultural expression and social inquiry[cite: 1]. More recently, my work has expanded to examine artificial intelligence, digital humanities, and the evolving relationship between technology, reading, and higher education[cite: 1].
