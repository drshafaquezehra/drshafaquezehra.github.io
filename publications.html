Edited Scholarly Book

Narrating the Everyday: Spaces, Practices and Voices

Authors Press, New Delhi | 2025
This volume, Narrating the Everyday: Spaces, Practices, Voices, seizes the generative framework with particular urgency in postcolonial and South Asian contexts, where the everyday bears the heavy burdens of empire’s afterlives, caste’s spectral persistence, partition’s fractures, and neoliberal acceleration. It exists to forge analytics from these textures, bridging cultural theory to lived urgencies – neoliberal platform drudgery, ecological disruptions, persistent hierarchies. It equips pedagogy with tangible entry points, activism with granular diagnostics, policy with traces of informal resilience, all drawn from literature’s attunement to residue and rhythm.
